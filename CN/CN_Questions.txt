1. What is computer network?

    A computer network in networking is a system where two or more computers or devices are connected together to share resources,
exchange data, and communicate with each other.

These devices can include computers, servers, smartphones, printers, and other hardware. 
The connection can be established using wired (like Ethernet cables) or wireless (like Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) technologies.

-> Purpose:

      - Share files, applications, and data.

      - Access shared hardware like printers, scanners, or storage.

      - Enable communication through emails, chats, or video calls.

       - Provide centralized storage and security.

-> Types of Networks:

      - LAN (Local Area Network): Covers a small area like an office, school, or home.

      - MAN (Metropolitan Area Network): Spans a city or large campus.

      - WAN (Wide Area Network): Covers a wide geographical area (e.g., the Internet).

-> Components:

         - Nodes/Devices: Computers, phones, servers.

        - Transmission Medium: Cables, fiber optics, radio waves.

        - Networking Devices: Routers, switches, hubs, modems.

       - Protocols: Rules for communication (e.g., TCP/IP, HTTP, FTP).
